AIDEN MURPHY

Boston, MA Congressional App Challenge 2026

ABOUT

Senior in high school. I ride my bike everywhere around Boston. Got real tired of hitting potholes and nearly wiping out on my way to school. So I built an app about it. That's basically the whole story.

When I'm not biking or coding I'm probably complaining about the T being late again.

THE APP

POTHOLEALERT lets cyclists and drivers report potholes by dropping a pin on a map. It aggregates reports, ranks the worst streets, and automatically generates complaint emails to the city.
BOSTON HAS OVER 19,000 POTHOLE COMPLAINTS PER YEAR. POTHOLEALERT HAS LOGGED 340+ IN BETA TESTING.

HOW IT WORKS

Built with React Native and Google Maps API. Users snap a photo of the pothole, the app geolocates it, and it goes into a public database. The app uses clustering to identify the worst intersections and generates a severity score based on report frequency and photos. City officials can log in to a dashboard to prioritize repairs.

It also has a "Dodge Mode" that warns cyclists about reported potholes on their current route. Because nobody wants a flat tire at 7:45am.
