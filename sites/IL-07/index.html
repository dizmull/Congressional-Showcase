About Me

What's good! I'm a sophomore and I play varsity basketball at my school in Chicago. I learned to code by watching YouTube tutorials at like 2am after practice. Turns out making apps is kinda like running plays -- you gotta plan it out, execute, and adapt when things go wrong.

HoopStats

HoopStats is a stat-tracking app built for high school basketball teams that can't afford expensive analytics software. You just tap the screen during games to log shots, rebounds, assists, and turnovers, and it generates full box scores and performance trends.

I built it because our coach was still using a clipboard and pen. Now three other schools in our conference use it too. It's built with vanilla JavaScript and saves everything locally so it works even in gyms with bad wifi.
