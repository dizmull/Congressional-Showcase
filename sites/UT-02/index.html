ABOUT ME

EAGLE SCOUT -- TROOP 417

I'm a junior at Highland High School in Salt Lake City, Utah. I hike every single weekend -- rain, snow, or shine. I earned my Eagle Scout rank last year, and my project was rebuilding a damaged section of trail in Big Cottonwood Canyon. That experience made me realize how many amazing trails are out there that people don't know about, or can't access because of poor information.

I want to make the outdoors accessible to EVERYONE, regardless of ability level, experience, or gear budget.

TRAILFINDER

HIT THE TRAIL

TrailFinder is a web app that helps people find hiking trails matched to their exact ability level. You input your fitness level, available time, gear, and any accessibility needs, and it recommends trails with detailed info including elevation profiles, surface type, water sources, and wheelchair-accessible sections.

I built it with Python (Flask) and JavaScript, using trail data from public land databases and user contributions. The accessibility filter is the feature I'm most proud of -- my younger cousin uses a wheelchair and she helped me test every part of it. Right now it covers 200+ trails in the Wasatch Range.
