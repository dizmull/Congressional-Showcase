About Me

Sophomore at Clark High School in Las Vegas. I'm an amateur astronomer with a 10-inch Dobsonian telescope set up on my roof. While everyone else in Vegas is looking at the neon lights, I'm looking at the stars. I've been stargazing since I was ten and I've logged over 300 observation sessions. My dream is to work at NASA — specifically on exoplanet detection. I'm also the founder of our school's Astronomy Club.

StarGazer

StarGazer is an app that helps amateur astronomers plan their observation sessions. It pulls real-time weather data, light pollution maps, and astronomical event calendars to tell you the best nights to go out and what to look for. Point your phone at the sky and it identifies constellations, planets, and deep-sky objects in augmented reality.

Built with Swift for iOS, using CoreLocation and ARKit. The app includes a personal observation journal where you can log what you've seen, attach photos, and track your progress toward spotting all 110 Messier objects.
